The best gifts for a senior with low vision or hearing loss aren't medical devices — they're the things that hand back a favorite activity: reading a novel, hearing a grandchild on the phone, following the TV without cranking the volume. Here are gifts, by need and budget, that genuinely land.

## How to choose a sensory-aid gift

<Callout type="tip" title="Lead with what it gives back">
Present these as gifts of connection and independence: "so you can read your mail again" or "so we can talk on the phone easily." The joy they restore is the whole point.
</Callout>

## For low vision

### Under $20 — an illuminated handheld magnifier

A bright, illuminated handheld magnifier is the most-used low-vision gift there is — perfect for prescriptions, labels, and the newspaper, and easy to keep by the reading chair.

### A bigger gift — an electronic video magnifier

For a senior with macular degeneration, an electronic magnifier with high zoom and high-contrast color modes (like white-on-black) can restore reading they'd given up. It's the gold standard low-vision specialists recommend.

## For hearing loss

### A conversation-saver — a personal sound amplifier

A simple body-worn amplifier with a big volume knob brings back conversations and TV, with no tiny buttons and very long battery life. The easiest hearing gift to actually use.

### A phone they can hear — an amplified phone

The telephone is where hearing loss hurts most. An [amplified large-button phone](/best/best-large-button-phones-for-seniors) boosts the earpiece far beyond a normal phone — and big, high-contrast keys help vision too.

## Gift pairings that work well

- **For a book lover losing their sight:** an electronic magnifier + a bright reading lamp.
- **For a parent who's withdrawn from phone calls:** an amplified or captioned phone + a personal amplifier.
- **For a grandparent who loves the news:** a page magnifier + a TV listening amplifier.

<Callout type="safety" title="Pair gifts with proper care">
These gifts help with daily tasks, but they don't replace professional care. Encourage regular eye exams and a hearing check — and for significant loss, a specialist can recommend the right prescription device.
</Callout>
