Coming home after hip, knee, or other surgery is when a senior is most at risk — and most in need of the right tools. These recovery essentials make bathing, dressing, and moving around safer while mobility is limited. Set the home up *before* discharge so everything is ready.

## Why each one helps

### For dressing without bending: sock aid + reacher

After hip or knee surgery, bending is restricted. A [sock aid](/best/best-reachers-for-seniors) lets a senior put socks on without reaching their feet, and a [reacher grabber](/reviews/rms-reacher-grabber-review) retrieves dropped items and clothing without bending — both are commonly recommended by occupational therapists.

<AffiliateButton id="rms-sock-aid" />

### For safer bathing: a shower chair or transfer bench

Bathing is the riskiest activity during recovery. A [shower chair](/reviews/medline-shower-chair-review) lets a senior bathe sitting down, and a [transfer bench](/reviews/drive-medical-transfer-bench-review) eliminates stepping over a tub wall — essential when one leg is healing.

<AffiliateButton id="medline-shower-chair" />

### For support: grab bars

A [grab bar](/best/best-grab-bars-for-seniors) at the shower entry and beside the toilet gives a stable handhold exactly where balance is hardest during recovery.

## A note on professional guidance

Before discharge, ask the surgical team or an occupational therapist which aids they recommend for your loved one's specific procedure — and follow any movement restrictions (like hip precautions) closely.
