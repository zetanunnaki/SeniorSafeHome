Recovering from surgery at home goes far better when the house is set up *before* your loved one comes home. The right equipment prevents falls, reduces strain on a healing body, and lets a senior do more for themselves — which speeds recovery and protects their dignity. This is the room-by-room checklist.

## The short answer

Cover four areas and you've handled most of recovery: **getting around** (wheelchair or walker), **the bathroom** (transfer bench/bath lift, raised toilet seat, grab bars), **the bedroom** (overbed table, bed rail, lighting), and **daily living** (a reacher so they don't bend or stretch). Tailor the specifics to the surgery.

## Getting around

A senior who tires easily or can't bear full weight needs a safe way to move through the house and to appointments.

1. **A [transport wheelchair](/best/best-transport-wheelchairs-for-seniors)** is light, folds for the car, and is pushed by a caregiver — ideal for appointments and longer distances during early recovery.
2. **A [walker or rollator](/best/best-walkers-rollators-for-seniors)** supports steadier walking as strength returns; a seated rollator lets them rest anywhere.

## The bathroom

The bathroom is the highest-risk room, and post-surgery precautions (especially after hip or knee replacement) make it critical.

- **A [tub transfer bench](/best/best-transfer-benches-for-seniors)** lets them sit and slide in without stepping over the tub wall.
- **A [bath lift](/best/best-bath-lifts-for-seniors)** lowers and raises them for a full bath if they can't do so safely themselves.
- **A [raised toilet seat](/best/best-raised-toilet-seats-for-seniors)** reduces how far they lower and lift — easing strain on hips and knees.
- **[Grab bars](/best/best-grab-bars-for-seniors)** at the shower and toilet give a stable handhold.

## The bedroom

Most recovery happens in bed, so make it a comfortable, functional base.

- **An [overbed table](/best/best-overbed-tables-for-seniors)** keeps meals, water, medications, a phone, and a laptop within reach — reducing how often they need to get up.
- **A [bed rail](/best/best-bed-rails-for-seniors)** gives something solid to grip for safer transfers in and out of bed.
- **[Motion-sensor night lights](/best/best-motion-sensor-night-lights-for-seniors)** light the path to the bathroom automatically.

## Daily living

Small aids protect a healing body from risky bending, reaching, and stretching.

- **A [reacher/grabber](/best/best-reachers-for-seniors)** retrieves dropped or high items without bending — important after hip surgery.
- **Dressing aids** (a sock aid, a long-handled shoehorn, a dressing stick) help them dress without straining.
- **A [pill organizer or automatic dispenser](/best/best-automatic-pill-dispensers-for-seniors)** keeps a busy post-op medication schedule on track.

## A simple recovery setup

Before your loved one comes home, have these ready:

1. **Mobility:** a transport wheelchair and/or walker.
2. **Bathroom:** transfer bench (or bath lift), raised toilet seat, grab bars.
3. **Bedroom:** overbed table, bed rail, night lights, a clear path to the bathroom.
4. **Daily living:** a reacher, dressing aids, and a medication plan.

<Callout type="safety" title="Follow your surgeon's precautions">
Every surgery has specific restrictions — weight-bearing limits, hip precautions, or activity rules. Your surgeon and physical therapist will tailor the equipment list to the procedure. When in doubt, ask before the discharge date.
