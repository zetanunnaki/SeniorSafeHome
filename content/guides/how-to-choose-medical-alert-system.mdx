A medical alert system can be the difference between a quick recovery and a long, dangerous wait on the floor. But the category is full of confusing plans and add-ons. This guide cuts it down to the decisions that actually matter, so you can choose confidently for an aging parent.

## The short answer

Match the system to the senior&apos;s lifestyle. **If they rarely leave home, an in-home system is cheapest and simplest. If they&apos;re active and go out, choose a mobile system with cellular and GPS.** Add automatic fall detection if they&apos;re at higher risk of falling, and make sure the device is comfortable enough that they&apos;ll actually wear it every day.

## Step 1: In-home vs. mobile

<Callout type="info" title="The core decision">
This single choice shapes everything else. In-home units are tied to a base station and a landline or cellular connection at home. Mobile units work anywhere on cellular and include GPS so responders can locate the wearer.
</Callout>

- **In-home** — Lowest cost, longest battery life, best for homebodies. The trade-off is no coverage away from home.
- **Mobile** — Go-anywhere protection with GPS. Best for active seniors. The trade-off is slightly higher cost and the need to recharge.

## Step 2: Decide on fall detection

Automatic fall detection can place a call even if the wearer can&apos;t press the button — valuable for anyone who has fallen before or has balance or fainting risks. It typically adds a few dollars a month. It isn&apos;t flawless (it can miss some falls or trigger false alarms), so treat it as a helpful backup to the manual button, not a replacement.

## Step 3: Choose a wearable they&apos;ll actually wear

The best system is the one your parent wears every day. Some seniors dislike the look of a medical pendant; for them, a discreet [smartwatch-style alert](/compare/medical-alert-pendant-vs-smartwatch) may win simply because it gets worn consistently. Weigh comfort, water-resistance (so it can be worn in the shower, where many falls happen), and battery life.

## Step 4: Understand the fees

Most monitored systems charge a monthly fee for 24/7 access to a response center. When comparing plans, check:

- The monthly monitoring cost and whether fall detection is included or extra
- Whether equipment is included or purchased separately
- Cancellation terms and whether contracts are required
- Battery life and how charging works (for mobile units)
