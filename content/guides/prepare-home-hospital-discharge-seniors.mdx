The first days home after a hospital stay are when a senior is most vulnerable — weak, possibly on new medications, and with movement restrictions. A little preparation **before discharge day** prevents falls, eases recovery, and reduces stress for everyone. Use this checklist.

## Before discharge: ask the care team

- Which **mobility aids and bathroom equipment** do they recommend?
- Are there **movement restrictions** (e.g., hip precautions — no bending past 90°)?
- Is **home health or outpatient therapy** ordered? Who schedules it?
- What are the **new medications, dosages, and follow-up appointments**?

## Set up the bathroom

Bathing is the riskiest activity during recovery — prepare it first:

1. **A [transfer bench](/best/best-transfer-benches-for-seniors)** if they bathe in a tub, so they never step over the wall while healing. A [shower chair](/best/best-shower-chairs-for-seniors) for a walk-in shower.
2. **[Grab bars](/best/best-grab-bars-for-seniors)** at the shower entry and beside the toilet.
3. **A [raised toilet seat](/best/best-raised-toilet-seats-for-seniors)** to reduce strain on healing hips and knees.
4. **A [non-slip bath mat](/best/best-non-slip-bath-mats-for-seniors).**

## Set up the bedroom and path

1. **Clear a wide, well-lit path** from the bedroom to the bathroom — remove rugs, cords, clutter.
2. **Add a [bed rail](/best/best-bed-rails-for-seniors)** and set the bed to a safe height.
3. **Place essentials within reach** — phone, water, glasses, medications, call button.
4. **Add motion-activated night lights** along the route.

## Gather recovery aids

If movement is restricted, these prevent dangerous bending and reaching:

- A **[reacher grabber](/best/best-reachers-for-seniors)** for dropped items and clothing.
- A **sock aid and dressing stick** for getting dressed without bending.
- A **[medical alert or caregiver call button](/best/best-medical-alert-systems-for-seniors)** so they can summon help.

## Handle medications and follow-up

- **Set up a [pill organizer](/best/best-pill-organizers-for-seniors)** with the new regimen before they're home.
- **Confirm follow-up appointments and transportation.**
- **Watch for warning signs** the discharge team flagged, and keep their number handy.

## A calm first day

<Callout type="tip" title="Do a dry run">
Before discharge, walk through the home yourself: sit on the bed, the shower bench, and the toilet; check that handles are solid and the path is clear. Fixing a gap now is far easier than during a 2 a.m. bathroom trip.
</Callout>
