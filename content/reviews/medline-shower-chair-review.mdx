The Medline Shower Chair with Back is the model we recommend to most families starting out with bathroom safety, and after using it in real bathrooms, it&apos;s easy to see why. It does the fundamentals right: it stays planted on a wet floor, supports the user comfortably, and gets out of the way when it&apos;s time to clean. It isn&apos;t fancy — and for a safety product, that&apos;s a feature, not a flaw.

## Why it works for seniors

The frame is wide and rust-proof aluminum, so it feels solid the moment you sit down — no creaking or flex. The four legs end in non-slip rubber tips that grip a wet shower floor, which is exactly where cheaper chairs fail. The backrest matters more than you&apos;d expect: for anyone who tires partway through a shower or has balance issues, having something to lean against turns a stressful task into a calm one.

Height adjusts from 16 to 21 inches without tools, so you can set it low enough that sitting down is easy or high enough that standing back up doesn&apos;t strain the knees.
