A **personal sound amplifier**, or **PSAP**, is a device that amplifies the sounds around the wearer — conversations, the television, a doorbell. It is **not** a medical device and is not intended to treat diagnosed hearing loss.

## How it differs from a hearing aid

A hearing aid is an FDA-regulated medical device tuned to a person's specific pattern of hearing loss, available over the counter for mild-to-moderate loss or by prescription for more complex cases. A PSAP simply makes nearby sound louder, without that customization. PSAPs are inexpensive and need no prescription, which makes them a practical first step for everyday listening — but for significant or one-sided hearing loss, an audiologist and a true hearing aid usually give better results.
