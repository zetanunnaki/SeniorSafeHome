**Fall detection** is a feature in some [medical alert systems](/categories/medical-alert-systems) that uses motion sensors to automatically detect a sudden fall and place an emergency call — without the wearer needing to press the help button.

## Why it matters

In a serious fall, a person may be unconscious, disoriented, or unable to reach the button. Automatic fall detection provides a safety net for exactly these situations, summoning a monitoring center or emergency contact on the wearer&apos;s behalf.

## What to know before relying on it

- **It&apos;s a backup, not a replacement.** No fall-detection system catches every fall, and some everyday motions can trigger false alarms. It works best alongside, not instead of, a manual help button.
- **It usually costs extra.** Most providers charge a small additional monthly fee for fall detection.
- **Wear consistency is key.** The feature only helps if the device is actually worn — including in the bathroom, where many falls happen.
