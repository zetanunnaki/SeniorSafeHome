In home safety, **bariatric** describes equipment built for **higher weight capacities and larger body sizes** than standard models. Where a typical shower chair or transfer bench supports 300–350 lb, bariatric versions are reinforced to hold **500 lb, 550 lb, or more**.

## What makes equipment bariatric

- **Higher weight rating** — usually 500 lb and up.
- **A wider, deeper seat** for comfort and stability.
- **Reinforced frames, crossbars, and legs** to handle the extra load without flexing.
- **Wider, more stable footprints** so the product doesn't tip.

## Why it matters

Using equipment near or beyond its weight limit is unsafe — frames can flex, legs can splay, and the product can fail at the worst moment. Choosing a bariatric model rated comfortably above the user's weight provides a stable, secure margin of safety.
