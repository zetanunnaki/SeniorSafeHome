**Macular degeneration** (often age-related macular degeneration, or AMD) is a common eye condition in older adults that affects the macula — the central part of the retina responsible for sharp, straight-ahead vision. It blurs or creates a blind spot in the center of vision while usually leaving peripheral (side) vision intact.

## What it means day to day

Because central vision handles reading and fine detail, macular degeneration makes tasks like reading the mail, recognizing faces, and seeing prescriptions difficult — even though a person can still navigate a room. **High contrast** helps more than magnification alone: electronic video magnifiers with high-contrast color modes (such as white text on a black background) are a gold standard recommended by low-vision specialists.

## Helpful aids

Illuminated and electronic [low-vision magnifiers](/best/best-low-vision-magnifiers-for-seniors), bright glare-free lighting, and large, high-contrast devices all help. See our guide to [low-vision aids for seniors](/guides/low-vision-aids-for-seniors). Macular degeneration should be diagnosed and managed by an ophthalmologist.
