A **grab bar** is a sturdy, wall-mounted handle that gives a person a stable handhold to steady themselves, transfer, or catch a fall. In senior home safety, grab bars are most often installed in the bathroom — the room where falls are most common.

## Two types, very different uses

- **Screw-mounted (weight-bearing):** Bolted into wall studs or solid blocking, these support a person's **full body weight** (typically rated to 500 lb) and can actually catch a fall. This is the type to use for real safety.
- **Suction-mounted:** Attach with suction cups to smooth tile. They provide **light balance support only** and must **never** be relied on to stop a fall.

## Where to install them

- **At the shower or tub entry** — for stepping in and out.
- **Inside the shower** — for support while standing and washing.
- **Beside the toilet** — for the sit-to-stand motion.

## Safe installation

A grab bar is only as strong as its mounting. Weight-bearing bars must be anchored into **studs or solid blocking** — never drywall alone.
