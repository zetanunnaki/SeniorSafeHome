A **personal emergency response system (PERS)** — commonly called a medical alert system — is a device that lets a senior call for help at the press of a button. It typically pairs a wearable pendant or wristband with a base unit or cellular connection that contacts a 24/7 monitoring center or designated contacts.

## How it works

When the button is pressed (or a fall is detected automatically), the system opens a two-way voice link to a monitoring operator, who assesses the situation and dispatches help — a family member, neighbor, or emergency services. In-home units use a base station; mobile units use cellular and GPS so help can find the wearer away from home.

## Who benefits most

A PERS is valuable for seniors living alone or at risk of falls, giving both the wearer and their family peace of mind. Many systems add automatic [fall detection](/glossary/fall-detection), which calls for help even if the person can't press the button.
