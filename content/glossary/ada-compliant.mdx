**ADA compliant** describes a product or installation that meets the standards of the **Americans with Disabilities Act** — a set of accessibility guidelines that, for home safety, most often apply to grab bars and bathroom layouts.

## What it means for grab bars

For a grab bar, "ADA compliant" generally signals that the bar:

- Can support significant weight (the ADA references a **250 lb** minimum load; quality home bars are rated to **500 lb**) **when properly installed into studs or solid blocking**.
- Has a graspable diameter (about **1.25–1.5 inches**) with adequate clearance from the wall (about **1.5 inches**).

## An important caveat

ADA compliance describes the **bar itself**. It only translates into real-world safety if the bar is **mounted into wall studs or solid backing** — a strong, ADA-rated bar screwed into drywall alone is not safe. The standard also assumes professional-grade installation.
