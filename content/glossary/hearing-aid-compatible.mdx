**Hearing aid compatible (HAC)** describes a phone that is designed to work cleanly with hearing aids — minimizing the buzzing, whistling, or interference that hearing-aid wearers often get with ordinary phones.

## What it means in practice

- **Acoustic coupling:** the phone's earpiece is shaped and tuned to work with a hearing aid's microphone.
- **Telecoil (T-coil) coupling:** many HAC phones support a hearing aid's **telecoil** setting, which picks up the phone's audio magnetically and bypasses background noise entirely — usually the clearest option for hearing-aid users.

## Why it matters for seniors

A senior who wears hearing aids may find a regular phone frustrating or unusable due to feedback. An HAC phone — especially a T-coil-compatible, [amplified](/best/best-large-button-phones-for-seniors) model — lets them hear calls clearly and stay connected and safe.
