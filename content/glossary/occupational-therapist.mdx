An **occupational therapist (OT)** is a licensed healthcare professional who helps people perform the everyday activities of daily living — bathing, dressing, cooking, moving around the home — safely and independently. For seniors, an OT is one of the most valuable resources for [aging in place](/glossary/aging-in-place).

## How an OT helps with home safety

- **Home safety assessment** — an OT can visit (or review) the home and identify fall hazards and barriers.
- **Equipment recommendations** — they suggest the right grab bars, shower chairs, transfer benches, raised toilet seats, and dressing aids for the person's specific abilities.
- **Technique training** — they teach safe ways to transfer, bathe, and dress, including precautions after surgery (such as hip precautions).

## When to involve one

Consider an OT assessment after a fall, a new diagnosis, a hospital discharge, or any noticeable change in mobility. A doctor can provide a referral, and the assessment is **sometimes covered by insurance or Medicare** — ask your provider.
