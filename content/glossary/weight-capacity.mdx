**Weight capacity** is the maximum load a piece of safety equipment — a shower chair, transfer bench, grab bar, raised toilet seat, or bed rail — is rated to support safely. It's one of the most important specifications to check before buying.

## Why it matters

Using equipment at or beyond its rated capacity is dangerous: frames can flex, legs can splay, and a product can fail at the worst possible moment. Always choose a rating **comfortably above** the user's body weight, not just equal to it, to leave a margin of safety.

## Typical ranges

- **Standard** shower chairs and benches: **300–350 lb**
- **Heavy-duty / [bariatric](/glossary/bariatric)** models: **500 lb and up**
- **[Grab bars](/glossary/ada-compliant):** **500 lb** when properly anchored into wall studs
- **Bed rails:** commonly **300–380 lb**

## A note on grab bars

A grab bar's high weight rating only holds if it's **mounted into studs or solid blocking**. The same bar screwed into drywall alone will not support anywhere near its rated capacity. See our [best grab bars guide](/best/best-grab-bars-for-seniors).
