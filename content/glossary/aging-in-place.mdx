**Aging in place** refers to a person&apos;s ability to live in their own home and community safely, independently, and comfortably as they grow older, regardless of age, income, or ability level. Rather than moving to assisted living or a nursing facility, an older adult adapts their existing home to meet changing needs.

## What aging in place involves

Successful aging in place usually combines several elements:

- **Home modifications** — grab bars, [shower chairs](/categories/shower-chairs), [raised toilet seats](/categories/raised-toilet-seats), improved lighting, and removing trip hazards.
- **Safety technology** — [medical alert systems](/categories/medical-alert-systems), fall detection, and easy-to-use [communication devices](/categories/large-button-phones).
- **Support services** — help with meals, transportation, housekeeping, or personal care as needed.

## Why it matters

Most older adults strongly prefer to remain in familiar surroundings near friends and routines. Thoughtful home safety upgrades — often modest and inexpensive — can make aging in place realistic for far longer by reducing the risk of falls and injuries that frequently trigger a move to a care facility.
