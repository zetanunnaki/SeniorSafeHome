A **rollator** is a type of walker with four wheels, hand-operated brakes, and — on most models — a padded seat and a storage pouch or basket. Unlike a standard walker, which must be lifted or pushed slightly with each step, a rollator rolls smoothly and continuously.

## How it differs from a walker

A standard walker has no wheels (or two front wheels) and is the most stable option, ideal indoors and for people who need to lean significant weight on the frame. A rollator trades a little of that stability for mobility: it glides over floors and pavement, its brakes let the user control speed and lock it in place, and its seat allows a rest during longer outings. Because the brakes must be squeezed, a rollator suits someone with enough hand strength to operate them.

## Who benefits most

Rollators are well suited to seniors who can walk but tire easily, need to rest frequently, or travel longer distances outdoors.
