Compression socks gently squeeze the legs to support circulation and reduce swelling — a real comfort for seniors who sit or stand for long periods, travel, or manage mild edema. We compared the best-rated options on compression level, material, fit, and comfort.

<Callout type="safety" title="Talk to a doctor first">
Compression socks are generally helpful, but firm (20–30 mmHg) compression isn't right for everyone. Check with a doctor or healthcare provider before using medical-grade compression, especially with diabetes, peripheral arterial disease, or other circulatory conditions.
</Callout>

## Which compression level?

- **15-20 mmHg (moderate)** — everyday wear, travel, mild swelling, standing all day.
- **20-30 mmHg (firm/medical-grade)** — varicose veins, post-surgery, edema. **Check with a doctor first.**

## How to choose

1. **Pick the compression level** for the need (moderate vs. firm) — and consult a doctor for firm.
2. **Choose the material** — merino wool for temperature/odor, copper or synthetic for value.
3. **Get the fit right** — measure the calf; wide-calf options exist (ACTINPUT).
4. **Look for a no-pinch top band** that won't roll or dig in.
