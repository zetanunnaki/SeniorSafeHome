For someone with dementia or memory loss, not knowing the day — or whether it's morning or night — is a genuine source of distress. A dementia day clock shows the full, unabbreviated day, date, and time-of-day phase in large, high-contrast type, plus alarms for medications and appointments. We compared the best-rated clocks on clarity, time-of-day display, reminders, and ease of setup.

<Callout type="tip" title="Spelled-out, not abbreviated">
The whole point is to remove confusion: look for a clock that displays **"TUESDAY, JUNE 14 — MORNING"** in full words, not "TUE 6/14." Abbreviations and small digits defeat the purpose.
</Callout>

## Which clock fits the need?

- **Core orientation** — a full spelled-out day/date with clear time-of-day phases.

- **Talking clock** — announces the time aloud for vision loss.
- **Reminder clock** — many alarms with medication and task icons.
- **Photo-frame combo** — familiar family faces to ease anxiety.

## How to choose

1. **Insist on spelled-out day/date** and a clear time-of-day phase.
2. **Size the display** to the room and the person's vision.
3. **Add the right reminders** — icon or text alarms for medications and meals.
4. **Check night behavior** — auto-dimming prevents both glare and total darkness.
