A power lift recliner gently tilts the whole chair forward to bring a senior to their feet, then reclines for all-day comfort — a major help for anyone who struggles to stand from a seated position. We compared the best-rated lift recliners and stand-assist lifts on lift capacity, recline range, controls, and comfort.

<Callout type="tip" title="Lift chair vs. stand-assist cushion">
A **lift recliner** is a full powered chair. A **stand-assist lift** is a motorized cushion that sits on the chair you already own and rises to help you up — cheaper, portable, and ideal if you don't want to replace your furniture.
</Callout>

## Which lift chair is right?

- **Simplest to use** — a 2-button, 3-position recliner with no massage electronics.
- **Most comfortable** — massage-and-heat models for back relief and long sitting.
- **Big & tall / bariatric** — wider seats and 450 lb+ capacity.
- **Lay-flat** — dual/triple-motor infinite-position chairs that recline flat to sleep.
- **Keep your chair** — a portable stand-assist cushion lift.

## How to choose

1. **Decide chair vs. cushion** — full lift recliner or a stand-assist cushion for your current chair.
2. **Match capacity and size** — confirm weight rating and seat width (big-and-tall options exist).
3. **Pick your features** — simple 2-button for reliability, or massage/heat/lay-flat for comfort.
4. **Plan the space** — lift recliners need clearance behind them to recline.

<Callout type="safety" title="Ask your care team">
For post-surgery recovery or chronic mobility conditions, your physician or occupational therapist can recommend the right chair position and features. Most lift chairs are FSA/HSA eligible, and a doctor's note may help with reimbursement.
</Callout>
