Most nighttime falls happen in the dark, on the way to the bathroom. A few well-placed motion-sensor night lights remove that risk entirely — the path lights up automatically, with no fumbling for a switch. They're inexpensive, and they're one of the highest-value fall-prevention upgrades you can make.

## Which type do you need?

- **Plug-in** (AUVON, MAZ-TEK, Sujeet, L LOHAS) — best near outlets: hallways, bathrooms, bedrooms. Never needs charging.
- **Rechargeable stick-on** (MCGOR, Gritin, ZioeYiue) — best where there's no outlet: closets, stairs, cabinets. Magnetic, no wiring.
- **Ceiling-mount** (WhitePoplar, Combuh) — best for overhead light in closets, pantries, or bathrooms.

## How to place them for fall prevention

<Callout type="safety" title="Light the whole path">
Put a light at the bedroom doorway, along the hallway, at the top and bottom of every staircase, and at the bathroom and kitchen entrances. The aim is simple: a senior should never take a step in the dark.
</Callout>

1. **Choose warm, glare-free light** — harsh white light wakes you up and dazzles tired eyes.
2. **Plug-in near outlets; rechargeable stick-on elsewhere.**
3. **Cover the stairs** at both ends — a dark stair is the single most dangerous spot.
