A grab bar is the highest-impact, lowest-cost fall-prevention upgrade you can make to a bathroom. The right bar gives a senior a stable handhold exactly where balance is hardest — stepping into the tub, standing up from the toilet, or steadying on a wet floor. The key is choosing a bar that matches both the wall and how much support the user actually needs.

## Where to place grab bars

1. **Beside the toilet** — a vertical or angled bar helps with the sit-to-stand motion that strains hips and knees.
2. **At the shower or tub entry** — a vertical bar gives something to hold while stepping over the edge.
3. **Inside the shower** — a horizontal bar offers support while standing and washing.

<Callout type="tip" title="Combine with a shower chair">
Grab bars work best alongside a stable [shower chair](/best/best-shower-chairs-for-seniors). Together they remove the two biggest bathroom hazards: slick footing and the strain of standing.
</Callout>
