A powered bath lift lets a senior who can't lower and raise themselves enjoy a full bath again — safely and independently, with no caregiver lifting. The seat descends into the tub at the push of a waterproof button and lifts back up when bathing is done. We compared the best-rated lifts on stability, waterproofing, battery life, and capacity.

<Callout type="tip" title="Bath lift or transfer bench?">
If the hardest part is *stepping over the tub wall*, a [tub transfer bench](/categories/transfer-benches) is simpler and cheaper. If the hardest part is *lowering and raising yourself* for a full soak, a bath lift is what you want.
</Callout>

## What to look for

- **Waterproof rating** — an IP-rated (e.g. IP68) design and waterproof controls for safe submerged use.
- **Stable base** — suction cups or a multi-point base that grips a wet tub or tile floor.
- **Weight capacity** — confirm it comfortably exceeds the user's weight (300–330 lb here).
- **Safety logic & battery** — enough charge per session, and a system that won't lower without power to raise back up.

## How to choose

1. **Confirm the need** — bath lift for lowering/raising; transfer bench for stepping over the wall.
2. **Check waterproofing** — insist on an IP-rated design and waterproof controls.
3. **Match the capacity** — comfortably above the user's weight.
4. **Mind storage** — foldable models (both picks here) tuck away between baths.
