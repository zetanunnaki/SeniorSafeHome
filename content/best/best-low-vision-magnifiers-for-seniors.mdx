## Which type of magnifier?

- **Handheld** — quick everyday tasks: prescriptions, labels, the newspaper.
- **Page / rechargeable** — longer reading sessions; larger lens, no battery cost.
- **Desk-lamp (hands-free)** — needlework, crafts, and repairs that need both hands.
- **Wearable headset** — sewing, jewelry, and detailed close work, hands free.
- **Electronic (video)** — severe low vision and macular degeneration; high zoom plus high-contrast color modes.

## How to choose

1. **Match magnification to the need** — 3X–6X for general reading; 10X+ or electronic for advanced low vision.
2. **Insist on good light** — bright, glare-free LEDs; multiple color modes are a bonus.
3. **Pick the form factor** — handheld for quick tasks, hands-free for crafts, electronic for severe loss.
4. **For macular degeneration, prioritize contrast** — high-contrast color modes help far more than magnification alone.
