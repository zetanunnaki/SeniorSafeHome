## How to choose a shower chair

<Callout type="tip" title="Measure first">
Measure the inside width of your shower or tub and the user's preferred seat height before buying. A chair that's too wide won't fit; one that's too tall makes sitting down harder.
</Callout>

1. **Match it to the bathing space.** Walk-in shower? A standard chair or bench works. Standard bathtub with a high wall? Strongly consider a [transfer bench](/best/best-transfer-benches-for-seniors).
2. **Check weight capacity** — choose a rating comfortably above the user's weight.
3. **Prioritize the feet** — non-slip rubber or suction tips keep the chair planted on a wet floor.
4. **Decide on back and arms** — a back adds security; armrests help with sitting and standing.

<Callout type="safety" title="Pair it with a grab bar">
A shower chair is safest combined with a properly installed [grab bar](/best/best-grab-bars-for-seniors) near the shower entry. Together they remove the two biggest bathroom hazards: slick footing and the strain of standing.
</Callout>
