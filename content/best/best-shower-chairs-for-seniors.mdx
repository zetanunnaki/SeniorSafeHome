A shower chair is one of the simplest, most effective ways to make bathing safer for an older adult. Sitting down removes the need to stand on a slick, wet surface, reduces fatigue, and gives a senior a stable base while washing. For many families, it&apos;s the first home safety product they buy — and the one that makes the biggest day-to-day difference.

After comparing dozens of models on stability, weight capacity, ease of cleaning, and comfort, these are the shower chairs we recommend most often.

If your loved one bathes in a standard bathtub with a high wall, the single most dangerous moment is stepping over that wall. ## How to choose a shower chair

<Callout type="tip" title="Measure first">
Before buying, measure the inside width of your shower or tub and the user&apos;s preferred seat height. A chair that&apos;s too wide won&apos;t fit, and one that&apos;s too tall makes sitting down harder.
</Callout>

1. **Match it to the bathing space.** Walk-in shower? A standard shower chair or bench works. Standard bathtub with a high wall? Strongly consider a transfer bench.
2. **Check weight capacity.** Choose a rating comfortably above the user&apos;s weight — most quality chairs support 300–350 lb.
3. **Prioritize the feet.** Non-slip rubber tips are what keep the chair planted on a wet floor.
4. **Decide on back and arms.** A back adds security for anyone who tires or has balance issues; armrests help with sitting and standing.
5. **Think about cleaning.** Drainage holes and smooth, non-porous surfaces resist mildew and are easier to keep clean.

<Callout type="safety" title="Pair it with a grab bar">
A shower chair is safest when combined with a properly installed grab bar near the shower entry. Suction bars are fine for light balance, but for full body-weight support, use a screw-mounted bar anchored into studs. See our [bathroom fall-prevention guide](/guides/prevent-bathroom-falls-seniors).
</Callout>
