Aging eyes need far more light — and better-quality light — to read and do close work comfortably. Natural-daylight LED lamps cut eye strain, reveal true colors for crafts, and make low-vision reading much easier, especially next to a [magnifier](/best/best-low-vision-magnifiers-for-seniors). We compared the best-rated desk and floor lamps on brightness, color rendering, glare control, positioning, and controls.

<Callout type="tip" title="Light is half the battle">
For low vision, good lighting often helps as much as magnification. Aim for **bright, natural daylight (5000–6000K)** with **high color rendering (90+ CRI)** and **flicker-free** light — and position it to the side to avoid glare.
</Callout>

## Desk lamp or floor lamp?

- **Desk / table lamp** — close work at a table, hobby desk, or bedside.
- **Floor lamp** — reading chairs and recliners with no table; aims over the shoulder.
- **High-CRI craft lamp** — true colors for needlework and detail work.
- **RG0 eye-safe** — extra caution after cataract surgery or with glare sensitivity.
- **2-in-1 light therapy** — bright task light that also lifts winter low mood.

## How to choose

1. **Go bright and daylight** — 5000–6000K with high CRI for comfort and true color.
2. **Match the form** — desk lamp for tables, floor lamp for reading chairs.
3. **Mind the eyes** — flicker-free, and RG0-certified for sensitive eyes.
4. **Ease the controls** — touch or remote for arthritic hands.
