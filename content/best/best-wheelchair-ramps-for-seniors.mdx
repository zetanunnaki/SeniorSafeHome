A ramp removes the bumps and steps that stop a wheelchair, walker, or scooter — at a raised doorway threshold, a single step, or a porch. We compared the best-rated rubber threshold ramps and portable folding aluminum ramps on rise height, width, weight capacity, and grip.

<Callout type="tip" title="Measure the rise first">
Before anything else, measure the **vertical rise** — the height from the ground to the top of the threshold or step. Everything else (ramp type and length) follows from that number. Longer ramps make a gentler, safer slope.
</Callout>

## Match the ramp to the rise

- **0–4" (a raised doorway track or low rise)** → a cuttable **rubber threshold ramp**.
- **2–6" (a single step or curb)** → a compact **2-ft folding ramp**.
- **4–12" (one or two steps)** → a **4-ft folding ramp**.
- **8–24" (a tall porch or stoop)** → an **8-ft folding ramp**.

## How to choose

1. **Measure the rise** and match it to the ramp type and length.
2. **Pick threshold vs. folding** — rubber threshold for doorways, folding aluminum for steps and travel.
3. **Check capacity** — confirm the rating exceeds the wheelchair plus rider.
4. **Mind the slope** — for permanent entrances, ask a CAPS or OT to size the ramp.
