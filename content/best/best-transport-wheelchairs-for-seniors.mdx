A transport wheelchair is the lighter, more compact cousin of a standard wheelchair — pushed by a caregiver, it folds small and lifts easily into a car, making appointments, errands, and travel far simpler. We compared the best-rated folding transport chairs on weight, seat comfort, brakes, capacity, and fold size.

<Callout type="tip" title="Transport vs. self-propelled">
A transport chair is **caregiver-pushed** (small rear wheels, lighter, folds smaller). If the user needs to wheel *themselves*, choose a self-propelled wheelchair with large 24-inch rear wheels instead.
</Callout>

## What matters most

- **Weight** — aluminum (15–22 lb) lifts easily; steel (25–38 lb) costs less but is heavier.
- **Brakes** — loop-lock parking brakes on most; caregiver squeeze-handbrakes help on hills.
- **Seat width & comfort** — 19 inches is standard; padded, desk-length armrests add comfort.
- **Fold size & capacity** — confirm it folds for your trunk and the capacity (250–300 lb) fits the user.

## How to choose

1. **Decide who steers** — transport (caregiver) vs. self-propelled (user wheels themselves).
2. **Prioritize weight if you lift often** — aluminum saves your back at the car.
3. **Match capacity and seat width** — 300 lb / 19 inches suits most; confirm for the user.
4. **Consider the terrain** — handbrakes and larger wheels help on hills and ramps.
