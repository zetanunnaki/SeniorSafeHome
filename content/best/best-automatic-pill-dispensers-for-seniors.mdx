An automatic pill dispenser does more than hold pills — it sounds an alarm at dose time, presents only the dose that's due, and (on smarter models) alerts a caregiver if one is missed. We compared the best-reviewed 28-day, locking, and app-connected dispensers on capacity, alarm clarity, locking security, and ease of refilling.

<Callout type="tip" title="Dispenser or organizer?">
If a loved one just needs help remembering, a [pill organizer](/categories/pill-organizers) may be enough. If they could take a wrong or double dose — or you need remote confirmation — an alarmed, locking automatic dispenser is the safer choice.
</Callout>

## How to choose

1. **Decide if you need locking** — essential for dementia or any double-dose risk.
2. **Match the alarm and capacity** — loud, repeating alarms and enough slots for the schedule.
3. **Consider connectivity** — app alerts let caregivers confirm doses from anywhere.
4. **Check outage protection** — battery backup for critical medications.
