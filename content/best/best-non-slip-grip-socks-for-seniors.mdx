Non-slip grip socks are one of the cheapest, easiest fall-prevention tools there is: silicone or rubber tread on the sole grips slick tile, hardwood, and stairs the way a bare or stocking foot never could. They're perfect for the risky barefoot moments — nighttime bathroom trips, cold floors, post-surgery shuffling. We compared the best-rated grip socks on grip, cushioning, cuff comfort, warmth, and ease of putting on.

<Callout type="tip" title="Why hospitals use them">
Grip socks are standard hospital issue precisely because slips on hard floors are a top fall risk. At home, they're cheap insurance for every barefoot trip — especially to the bathroom at night.
</Callout>

## How to choose

1. **Prioritize full tread** — more sole coverage means better grip.
2. **Match the cuff to the foot** — wide, non-binding cuffs for swollen feet.

4. **Think about putting them on** — easy-on cuffs help arthritic hands and caregivers.
