Getting into and out of a car is one of the hardest, highest-strain transfers a senior makes — and a frequent source of injury after hip or knee surgery. The right aids make it manageable. We compared the best-rated swivel cushions, car-door grab handles, and leg lifters on stability, capacity, and ease of use.

<Callout type="safety" title="After hip surgery, check your precautions">
A swivel cushion is invaluable post-op because it lets you turn without twisting the hip — but only within your surgeon's limits. Confirm your rotation precautions with your surgeon or physical therapist before using one.

## Which aid do you need?

- **Grab handle** — a sturdy handhold to push up from; latches into the door frame.
- **Swivel cushion** — rotates the body out without twisting the hip (post-hip surgery).
- **Leg lifter** — moves a weak or post-op leg in and out without bending.
- **A common post-op kit** — a grab handle + a leg lifter, or a swivel cushion + a leg lifter.

## How to choose

1. **Match aids to the need** — handle for grip, swivel for turning, leg lifter for the leg.
2. **Check capacity and fit** — 300–400 lb handles; loops that fit a cast.
3. **Build the post-op kit** — most people pair two aids (e.g. swivel + leg lifter).
4. **Mind precautions** — confirm hip rotation limits before using a swivel cushion.
