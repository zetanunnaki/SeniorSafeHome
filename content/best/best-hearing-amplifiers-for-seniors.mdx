A personal sound amplifier makes everyday sounds clearer and louder — conversations, the TV, a doorbell — without the cost of prescription hearing aids. We compared the best-reviewed behind-the-ear, in-ear, and pocket amplifiers on amplification, noise reduction, battery life, and how easy they are to operate.

<Callout type="safety" title="Amplifiers vs. hearing aids">
Most products here are **personal sound amplifiers (PSAPs)** — they amplify all sound and are **not** FDA-cleared medical hearing aids. They're great for everyday listening, but for diagnosed or significant hearing loss, see an audiologist; a fitted hearing aid may serve you better.
</Callout>

## Which style fits you?

- **Pocket / body-worn** — biggest, simplest controls and longest battery; great for TV and one-on-one talk.
- **Behind-the-ear (BTE)** — more discreet, rechargeable, good all-day battery; some come as a pair.
- **In-ear** — the most discreet; best for mild amplification and quiet settings.

## How to choose

1. **Decide on style** — pocket for simplest use, BTE/in-ear for discretion.
2. **Check the controls** — big knobs are far easier than tiny buttons for older hands.
3. **Look for noise reduction + a directional mic** — clearer speech in busy rooms.
4. **Know the limits** — for diagnosed hearing loss, consult an audiologist.
