## Walker vs. rollator: which do you need?

- **Standard walker** — four legs you lift or slide; the most stable, best if you lean your weight on it. Our bariatric pick is a heavy-duty walker.
- **Rollator** — four wheels, brakes, and a seat; rolls continuously, best if you walk steadily but need balance support and a place to rest.

## How to choose

1. **Walker or rollator** based on whether you lean your weight (walker) or need rolling support with a seat (rollator).
2. **Wheel size** — 6" for indoors, 7.5–8" for outdoor and uneven ground.
3. **Weight** matters if you'll lift it into a car — aluminum (9.5–13 lb) beats steel (18+ lb).
4. **Brakes** — Feather Touch / low-grip brakes are best for arthritis.
5. **Capacity & width** — bariatric models reach 500 lb; petite models suit shorter users and narrow homes.

<Callout type="tip" title="Get the height right">
Set the handles so the senior's elbows bend about 15° while standing tall with hands on the grips. Too low causes hunching; too high causes shoulder strain.
</Callout>
