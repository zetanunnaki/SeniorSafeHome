Raising a bed, sofa, or chair just a few inches can make getting up far easier on aging knees and hips — and a higher bed can be matched to wheelchair height for safer, level transfers. Risers need no tools and are completely reversible. We compared the best-rated risers on lift height, weight capacity, stability, and extras.

<Callout type="tip" title="Match the height to the goal">
For **easier standing**, raise the bed until the knees sit roughly level with the hips. For **wheelchair transfers**, match the bed surface to the wheelchair seat. If the bed ends up high, add a step stool for safe entry.
</Callout>

## Which riser do you need?

- **Stackable adjustable** — choose 3", 5", or 8" as needs change.
- **Heavy-duty / bariatric** — thousands of pounds of capacity for reinforced beds.
- **With USB & outlets** — keep a phone, CPAP remote, or hearing-aid charger bedside.
- **Fixed height** — a simple, rock-solid single lift.
- **Step stool** — safe entry once the bed is raised.

## How to choose

1. **Pick the height** — adjustable sets let you fine-tune; fixed risers are simplest.
2. **Check the capacity** — well above furniture plus the heaviest user.
3. **Confirm leg fit** — recessed cups or L-lips hold the legs securely.
4. **Add a step stool** if the raised bed is tall.
