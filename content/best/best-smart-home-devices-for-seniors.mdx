Smart-home technology can genuinely help with aging at home: hands-free voice control for limited mobility, video calling that fights isolation, smart lighting that prevents falls in the dark, and reminders for medications. We compared the most senior-friendly voice assistants, displays, a senior tablet, and accessories on ease of use, connection features, and safety value.

<Callout type="tip" title="Start simple, then add">
Begin with a **voice speaker** (just say "Alexa") before adding screens. From there, a **display** enables video calls and **smart switches** handle lighting. A caregiver usually does the one-time setup.
</Callout>

## Which device for which need?

- **Easiest start** — a screen-free smart speaker for voice reminders and calls.
- **Video calling** — a smart display with an auto-framing camera and photo frame.
- **Avoids tech confusion** — a senior-specific tablet, scam-proof and family-managed.
- **Fall-prevention lighting** — voice/app smart switches, scheduled at sunset.
- **Reuse existing devices** — an IR hub that voice-controls the current TV and AC.

## How to choose

1. **Start with voice** — a speaker is the simplest, cheapest entry.
2. **Add a screen** for video calls and photos.
3. **Address tech anxiety** with a senior-specific, scam-proof tablet.
4. **Improve safety** with smart lighting and voice control of existing devices.
